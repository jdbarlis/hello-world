Introduction

Peñaranda, a delightful gem in Nueva Ecija, Philippines, is where history, culture, and small-town charm come together. Brimming with well-preserved heritage sites, lively festivals, and warm local traditions, this picturesque destination invites travelers to explore its scenic corners, capture stunning photos, and experience the authentic heart of Filipino culture. Whether you're a fan of history, nature, or simply discovering hidden gems, Peñaranda makes every visit feel special.

History

Founded in the 19th century, Peñaranda preserves several heritage sites including St. Francis of Assisi Church and the iconic Peñaranda Arch. Its historic irrigation system, the Penris, continues to support the town’s agricultural livelihood.

Activities

Visitors can enjoy walking tours around the heritage sites, take photographs at the scenic irrigation system, and sample local delicacies at the food park. Festivals and cultural events also offer an immersive experience for tourists.

Visitor Information

Best time to visit: All year round, especially during local festivals.
Location: Peñaranda, Nueva Ecija, Philippines.
Accessibility: Easily reachable by road from major towns and cities in Central Luzon.
